Per the research: lead with this in **Featured (position 1)** *and* add it as a **Projects**
entry tied to your Penn State / a finance club, so it shows up high *and* reads as credible.
Frame it around the decision it answers and the analyst / risk / M&A keywords — not the code.

---

## 1) Featured section  →  Add → "Add a link"

Paste your live Streamlit URL, then use this title + description:

**Title**
```
Financial Health & Red-Flag Screener (Python)
```

**Description**
```
A tool I built that reads a public company's financial statements and returns a
fast read on three questions analysts and deal teams actually ask: How close is it
to distress? How strong are the fundamentals? Do the earnings look clean — or
manipulated?

It runs three published models — the Altman Z-Score (distress risk), the Piotroski
F-Score (fundamental strength), and the Beneish M-Score (earnings-manipulation red
flags, the model famously used to flag Enron) — and turns raw filings into a
buy / watch / avoid call. Built in Python; try it on any public ticker.
```

## 5) Talking points (so you can speak to it in interviews / coffee chats)

- **Purpose / the decision it answers:** "I wanted a fast way to decide whether a company
  is worth deeper diligence — is it financially healthy, and can I trust the earnings?
  So I built a screen around the decision, not the other way around."
- **Why three models (the versatility angle):** Z-Score = distress/credit risk, F-Score =
  fundamental quality, M-Score = earnings integrity. Together they cover the **financial
  analyst**, **risk**, and **M&A diligence** lenses in one tool.
- **The fraud angle, framed as finance:** the Beneish M-Score detects the statistical
  fingerprints of earnings manipulation (ballooning receivables, soaring accruals) — it's a
  finance model from the *Financial Analysts Journal*, famously used to flag **Enron**. That's
  how I keep my financial-crime interest grounded in financial analysis.
- **What you learned:** how the three statements connect, what manipulation looks like in the
  numbers, and how to ship a real, deployed Python app end-to-end.
- **Honest framing:** "It's a screen, not a verdict — a starting point for diligence." (Shows
  judgment; recruiters respect not overclaiming.)
